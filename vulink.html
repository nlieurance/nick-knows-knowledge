Overview

VuLink is a telemetry device used in water monitoring. It connects to a data logger that records temperature, pH, and other measurements — then uploads that data to the cloud. The project encompassed the physical device, a mobile app, and a web app. I owned the full content lifecycle: from UI terminology and in-box documentation to online FAQs and post-launch maintenance.

The Challenge

Environmental scientists often install telemetry systems in remote areas where access is difficult and costly. A setup error might mean hours of driving to fix — or even a helicopter ride. The question was: how could we give users the confidence to deploy a complex telemetry system, in the field, with minimal support?

Business Goals

In-Situ had been reselling another company's telemetry devices for years, but reliability was poor and usability was worse. VuLink was an opportunity to boost telemetry sales, reduce tech support calls, and prove that good content design could be a meaningful part of a hardware product's success.

Process

Cross-functional Partnership

My work began at project kickoff. I participated in daily standups and weekly syncs to keep documentation aligned with product development in real time. When engineers discovered that incorrect battery installation could cause a fire, the UX designer and I developed a two-part solution: a warning sticker in the battery compartment and a prominent graphic in the quickstart guide. After launch, I continued to incorporate feedback from sales and support teams into new online FAQs.

Defining Terminology

One of the most impactful parts of my role was translating internal jargon into plain, user-centered language. Each change required aligning engineering, product, and support teams around new terminology before it could ship.

End of session
Short text explanation
Engineers used "end of session" for disconnection. Users didn't share that mental model.
Reporting
Uploading
Support confirmed "reporting" already had a different, specific meaning to customers.
Downstream instrument
Connected instrument
In water monitoring, "downstream" is literal. The internal term was a collision waiting to happen.

Writing Documentation

I designed a four-page quickstart guide — printed on two pieces of paper — with the goal of making installation nearly foolproof for field technicians. Step-by-step instructions with photos reduced the cognitive load of setup in unfamiliar environments. For users with advanced needs, I also produced a full PDF manual covering technical specs and edge cases.

Results

VuLink became the fastest-selling device in In-Situ's history, reaching 400% of its first-year sales forecast. Out-of-box failures due to user error came in at under 1%.

"The usability is out of sight. It often surprised me how every aspect of VuLink was so well thought out."
