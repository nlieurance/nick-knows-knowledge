Overview

In-Situ had no centralized, searchable knowledge base. Support requests were handled reactively, documentation was scattered, and there was no way to measure what users actually needed. My mandate: build a governed knowledge system from scratch — covering requirements gathering, platform evaluation, information architecture, content authoring, launch, and ongoing governance.

The Challenge

In-Situ's users ranged from high-school graduates with no formal training to PhD scientists. Any knowledge system had to serve all of them without overwhelming anyone — and it had to scale as the product line grew, without requiring proportional headcount increases.

Business Goals

Reduce inbound support volume. Give support reps a reliable shared reference. Create a measurable content foundation that could grow with new product launches.

Process

Gathering Requirements

I surveyed internal stakeholders — support reps, product managers, and field-facing teams — to identify must-have features and common user pain points. I also conducted user interviews. The finding was consistent: people needed self-service options. And from the start, I built in two governance requirements: user feedback on individual articles, and engagement analytics. Without those signals, there's no way to know what's working or what needs to be fixed.

Evaluating Platforms

I evaluated three approaches before deciding:

  • MindTouch — turned PDFs into searchable content, but onboarding was tedious, authoring was clunky, and pricing was steep.
  • Zendesk — solid platform, but larger and more complex than the project warranted.
  • WordPress — maximum authoring control, built-in analytics, user feedback plugins, and maintainable by one person. The right tool for the scope.

Building a Prototype

Before committing to a full launch, I built a fully functioning prototype on Google Cloud Console. Stakeholders could visit, search, and navigate as if it were the real product — so feedback was grounded in actual use, not imagination.

Launching & Governing

I worked alongside the marketing team redesigning the corporate site to align branding, collaborated with In-Situ's web developer on the technical integration, and embedded the knowledge base in the Support menu for discoverability. I also established an ongoing maintenance cadence — monitoring article ratings, tracking engagement data, and updating content as products evolved. Governance wasn't an afterthought; it was part of the design from day one.

Results

The knowledge base now serves hundreds of users every week. Support call volume decreased, and support reps adopted the FAQs as a go-to reference during live calls — an unplanned but telling outcome. In-Situ gained visibility into content performance for the first time: article ratings, engagement data, and search patterns that informed what to build and maintain next.
